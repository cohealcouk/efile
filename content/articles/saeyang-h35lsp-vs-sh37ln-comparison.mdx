# Saeyang H35LSP vs SH37LN: Head-to-Head Technical Comparison

## Executive Summary

Saeyang offers two distinct handpiece options for their Marathon series: the H35LSP (standard) and SH37LN (enhanced). This comprehensive comparison analyzes the technical differences, performance characteristics, and clinical applications to help UK professionals make informed decisions.

## Technical Specifications Comparison

### H35LSP (Standard Handpiece)
- **Torque**: 4.2 Ncm
- **Speed Range**: 0-28,000 RPM
- **Vibration**: 2.8 m/s²
- **Weight**: 165g
- **Length**: 145mm
- **Grip**: Standard silicone
- **Bur Lock**: Manual twist-lock

### SH37LN (Enhanced Handpiece)
- **Torque**: 4.5 Ncm
- **Speed Range**: 0-30,000 RPM
- **Vibration**: 2.3 m/s²
- **Weight**: 180g
- **Length**: 148mm
- **Grip**: Enhanced ergonomic silicone
- **Bur Lock**: Quick-release system

## Performance Analysis

### Torque Delivery
The SH37LN delivers 7% more torque (4.5 Ncm vs 4.2 Ncm), which becomes noticeable during demanding applications like thick acrylic removal or callus debridement.

### Vibration Control
Both handpieces perform well within clinical safety standards, but the SH37LN's enhanced damping system reduces vibration by 18% compared to the H35LSP.

### Ergonomics
The SH37LN features an improved grip design with better weight distribution, though it's slightly heavier. The H35LSP remains popular for its lighter weight during extended use.
