# Torque vs RPM: Why Saeyang Marathon Outperforms Cheap E-Files

## Executive Summary

Many nail technicians mistakenly believe that higher RPM automatically means better performance. However, torque is the critical factor that determines real-world cutting power. Saeyang Marathon e-files deliver superior torque that enables efficient material removal even at moderate RPM settings, while budget e-files with high RPM but low torque struggle with demanding applications.

## Understanding Torque vs RPM

### What is RPM (Revolutions Per Minute)?
RPM measures how fast the bur spins - the rotational speed. While important, RPM alone doesn't indicate cutting power.

### What is Torque?
Torque measures the rotational force - the actual cutting power. Higher torque means the bur maintains speed under load and can cut through tough materials without stalling.

## The Critical Relationship

### Power Formula
```
Power (Watts) = Torque (Nm) × Angular Velocity (rad/s)
```

### Real-World Impact
- **High RPM + Low Torque**: Fast spinning but stalls easily under load
- **Moderate RPM + High Torque**: Maintains speed under load, efficient cutting
- **High RPM + High Torque**: Optimal performance (expensive combination)

## Why Budget E-Files Fail

### Marketing Deception
Budget manufacturers advertise high RPM numbers (30,000-35,000 RPM) but fail to mention inadequate torque. This creates a false impression of capability.

### Motor Quality Issues
- **Brushless vs Brushed**: Budget e-files often use cheaper brushed motors
- **Power Supply**: Limited current delivery reduces torque under load
- **Heat Management**: Poor cooling causes power reduction during use

### Real Performance Problems
- **Speed Droop**: RPM drops 40-60% under load
- **Stalling**: Complete stoppage with moderate pressure
- **Inconsistent Cutting**: Uneven material removal
- **Increased Vibration**: Motor strain causes excessive vibration
